Introduction

An automatic street light system using DIAC and TRIAC is an efficient lighting solution designed to automatically control streetlights based on ambient light levels. This system eliminates the need for manual switching, ensuring energy savings and improved functionality. The circuit operates on 220V AC and utilizes a light-dependent resistor (LDR) to detect changes in natural light. When the surrounding light intensity drops (e.g., at dusk), the LDR triggers the circuit, activating the DIAC-TRIAC combination to switch on the streetlight. At dawn, when light levels increase, the system automatically turns off the lights, conserving energy. The DIAC (Diode for Alternating Current) and TRIAC (Triode for Alternating Current) components play a key role in controlling the AC power to the streetlight, ensuring smooth and efficient switching. This system is widely used in urban lighting, highways, and smart city applications, offering a cost-effective and automated street lighting solution.

Working Principle

The system works by detecting ambient light levels using an LDR (Light Dependent Resistor) and controlling a DIAC-TRIAC circuit to switch the streetlight on or off automatically. During the day, the LDR has low resistance, preventing the TRIAC from turning on the light. At night, when light levels drop, the LDR’s resistance increases, triggering a voltage change in the circuit. This activates the DIAC, which provides a pulse to the TRIAC, allowing AC power to flow and turning on the streetlight. In the morning, as light increases, the LDR’s resistance decreases, stopping the DIAC from triggering the TRIAC, which turns off the streetlight. This system enables automatic operation and energy efficiency without manual control. The components are:

  • LDR (Light Dependent Resistor):Senses ambient light levels. It has low resistance in daylight (keeping the light off) and high resistance at night (activating the circuit).
  • Resistors & Capacitors Form a voltage divider circuit to regulate the triggering voltage for the DIAC.
  • DIAC (Diode for Alternating Current): Acts as a triggering device for the TRIAC, allowing it to switch on the AC load.
  • TRIAC (Triode for Alternating Current): Functions as an electronic switch, controlling 220V AC power to turn the streetlight on or off.
  • Streetlight (AC Lamp): The final load that is switched on/off automatically based on light levels.
