Introduction

A fire fighting robot is an autonomous system designed to detect and extinguish fires automatically. it uses sensors such as flame or temperature sensors to identify fire sources and activates a response mechanism, such as a water pump or extinguisher, to control the fire. the robot can navigate through an environment, avoiding obstacles while reaching the fire location. it is useful in hazardous situations where human intervention is risky, making it suitable for industrial safety, rescue operations, and smart fire prevention systems.

Working Principle

The fire fighting robot works by continuously monitoring its surroundings using fire detection sensors such as flame sensors or temperature sensors. when a fire is detected, the robot processes the sensor data and determines the fire's location. it then moves towards the fire using motorized wheels while avoiding obstacles with the help of ultrasonic sensors. once it reaches the fire source, the robot activates its extinguishing mechanism, which can be a water pump, co2 extinguisher, or fan-based system. after extinguishing the fire, it continues scanning for any remaining threats. this system operates autonomously, reducing the risk to human firefighters and ensuring quick fire suppression.. The components are:

  • Flame sensor or temperature sensor: Detects the presence of fire by sensing heat or flames and sends signals to the controller.
  • Microcontroller (Arduino or ESP32) : Processes data from sensors, controls movement, and activates the fire extinguishing system.
  • Motor drivers and DC motor: Enable the robot to move towards the fire location with proper direction control.
  • Water Pump: Activates when a fire is detected to spray water or gas to extinguish the flames.
  • Battery or power supply:Provides necessary power to all components for continuous operation.

Applications

  • Industrial fire safety: Used in factories and warehouses to detect and extinguish fires before they spread, reducing damage and risk to workers.
  • Rescue operations: Deployed in hazardous environments where human firefighters cannot reach, such as collapsed buildings or chemical fires.
  • Smart home and office fire prevention : Integrated into automated fire safety systems to detect and control fires in residential and commercial spaces.
